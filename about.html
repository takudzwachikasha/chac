General Information About CHAC

The inaugural Climate and Health Africa Conference (CHAC) is a crucial and timely event that signifies Africa's entry into the global dialogue on the intersection of climate change and health. The conference aims to address the urgent need to tackle the detrimental health impacts of climate change in Africa, where the burden of climate-sensitive diseases is disproportionately high, and adaptive capacities are limited. By incorporating evidence-based interdisciplinary strategies, CHAC 2024 seeks to catalyze an African Health and Climate Declaration, integrating cutting-edge research into tailored adaptation and mitigation approaches for the continent's health systems.

CHAC 2024 serves as a platform for sharing innovations, best practices, and solutions that enhance climate resilience in health. It will guide Africa in redefining its approach to health within the context of climate change, fostering a stronger presence on the global stage. The conference's outcomes will include scientific publications, a comprehensive African Health and Climate Declaration, action plan blueprints for implementation, and the announcement of new partnerships and funding commitments.

The evaluation of abstracts is overseen by a distinguished scientific committee composed of senior researchers, chief editors of journals, and policy makers specializing in the field of climate change and health. Participation in the conference is open to researchers, professionals, experts, students, and anyone passionate about the intersection of climate change and health. Scholarships are also provided to eligible individuals who meet the criteria outlined in the scholarship guidelines.

Funding and Grant Support

Wellcome Trust provides significant support to CHAC 2024. In addition, Wellcome Trust offers funding to eligible policy makers and early career researchers, which includes coverage for travel, accommodation, and conference expenses. The registration fees paid by participants cover conference materials, refreshments, and lunches.
