    Poster presentation guidelines

    1. Poster Size and Format
      • Design your poster to fit within the specified dimensions provided by the conference organizers. (size A0)
      • Write your abstract number as shown in the sample poster.
      • Ensure the font size is legible from a reasonable distance.
    2. Content and Structure:
      • Clearly state the title of your research or project at the top of the poster.
      • Organize the content in a logical sequence, including sections such as Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion, and Conclusion.
      • Use headings, subheadings, and bullet points to make the information easily readable and visually appealing.
      • Include relevant figures, tables, graphs, or images that support and enhance the understanding of your work.
      • Highlight key findings, significant results, and important conclusions.
    3. Visual Appearance:
      • Use a clean and uncluttered design that attracts attention and facilitates comprehension.
      • Choose colours, fonts, and background elements that are visually appealing and easy to read.
      • Use high-resolution images and graphics for clear representation.
      • Ensure a balanced distribution of text and visuals throughout the poster.
    4. Poster Presentation Session:
      • Be present at your poster during the assigned presentation session.
      • Engage with attendees, answer questions, and provide additional information.
      • Prepare a short summary or elevator pitch to deliver to interested participants.
